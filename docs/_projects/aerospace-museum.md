> The Aerospace VR Experience is an immersive and interactive virtual reality environment developed in collaboration with the Aerospace Museum of California. The experience places users in the role of a space technician traveling to a space station to repair critical damage caused by a recent solar flare. Through engaging missions and hands-on problem solving, the project aims to inspire curiosity about space exploration while fostering interest in STEM fields! 

<cite>Aerospace Museum of California Virtual Reality Experience</cite> --- LAES Cal Poly, 2026
{: .small}

**Features:**

* VR locomotion built around jetpack-style movement, braking, and snap turning
* Guided dialogue and scene actions for mission flow and tutorialization
* Multiple interactive puzzle sequences, including:
  * Wire Repair
  * Color-sequence authentication
  * Crack tracing / epoxy repair

Aerospace VR Experience was made for the Meta Quest 2/3/Pro and built in Unity.

The Aerospace VR experience was my senior project as an undergraduate. My role on the project was to fix various bugs and analyze and enhance the experience to make sure it's fun and engaging throughout. I mainly worked on the wire repair puzzle where I implemented some tweaks such as a clear light indicator to guide the player as we found many players during our playtest were completing the puzzle in the wrong order which led to some bugs.

Personally, I hadn't worked in a semi-large group before for a game (10 students) so it was intimidating at first. Weekly meetings, giving updates, working and communicating with different people, it felt like working in a small game studio as everyone had their own roles.

During my last quarter at Cal Poly, we were able to go on a field trip to conduct a playtesting session at the Aerospace Museum of California. Prior to that, we had playtesting sessions hosted at Cal Poly to prepare us for the session at the museum. Along the way we modified the game according to feedback we received. Once we arrived at the museum and began playtesting, the biggest thing I noticed was that the age range of playtesters was huge. This made me realize how experiences like these must accomodate for different people. Mainly that accessibility features must be implemented to accomodate for anybody and everybody.

Ultimately, the participants at the museum gave us a lot of feedback on the experience and showed that project still has a long way to go. I got to meet a lot of cool people and learned a lot throughout my time working on the project!
